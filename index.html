🎴 Full Arcana Card Game

Basic Rules

🏆 Welcome

Welcome to Full Arcana, a turn-based card game where strategy and class abilities decide your path to victory. Here’s everything you need to dive into the gameplay!

❤️ 1. Hit Points (HP)

Definition: HP represents your character’s life force. If your HP drops to 0, you’re out of the game.
Starting HP: Every class begins with 100 HP unless specified otherwise by class mechanics or effects.
Damage: Incoming attacks reduce HP. Certain effects may cause healing to restore lost HP but cannot exceed your starting HP unless explicitly stated.
Death: Once your HP hits 0, you cannot take any further actions.

⚡ 2. Action Points (AP)

Definition: AP is the currency used to play cards. Each card has an AP cost, which must be paid to activate it.
Starting AP: All players begin with 7 AP unless their class specifies otherwise.
Regeneration: At the start of each turn, your AP resets to its maximum value.
Overload: If you attempt to spend more AP than you have, penalties or specific mechanics may apply (e.g., damage to self).

🃏 3. Drawing Cards

Hand Limit: Players may hold up to 10 cards by default, but class abilities or effects can modify this limit.
Draw Phase: At the start of the game, draw 5 cards and up to 2 cards at the start of your turn unless otherwise stated.
Deck Management: When your deck runs out of cards, shuffle your discard pile to create a new deck. Certain classes (like the Necromancer) can manipulate this pile for strategic advantage.

⏱️ 4. Card Timing

Cards have different timing windows, affecting when they can be used:
Normal/Cast: These cards are played during your turn.
Quick: These cards can be played during any enemy’s turn when targeting you or an ally.
Permanent: These cards remain in play indefinitely, providing ongoing effects.

🎲 5. Initiation Roll and Turn Order

Initiation Roll: At the start of each turn, all players roll a die (D20). The player with the highest roll chooses their position in the turn order (e.g., 1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc.). The second-highest roller selects next, and so on.
Dynamic Play: Certain cards or effects (like the Wizard’s Temporal Manipulations) may change the turn order mid-game, adding strategic depth.

🚀 6. Ready to Play?

Now you’re ready to dive into the game and put your strategies to the test! Use the companion app to track your stats, manage resources, and keep the gameplay smooth, totally optional.
