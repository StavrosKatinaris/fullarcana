🎴 Full Arcana Card Game

Advanced Rules

🎯 Objective

Players compete individually or in teams, using unique class-based decks. The goal is to eliminate opponents by reducing their HP to zero or to outlast all other players/teams. The last player or team remaining with HP wins the game.

🛠️ Game Setup

  • 📜 Deck Selection: Choose a class-based deck aligned with a character archetype (e.g., Warrior, Mage, Rogue). Each deck has 50 cards designed for that class’s strengths and playstyle.
  • ❤️ Health Points (HP): Start with 100 HP. Track HP using tokens, counters, or a score sheet.
  • ⚡ Action Points (AP): Start each turn with 7 AP to spend on card actions. Cards indicate their AP cost.
  • 🃏 Initial Hand: Draw 5 cards at the start of the game.

📜 Additional Rules

  • 🤝 Team Play: In team mode, players can strategize with teammates, but AP cannot be shared.
    Each player chooses to be 'Frontline' or 'Backline':
    • Frontline: Can be targeted by all attacks.
    • Backline: Cannot be targeted by melee single-target attacks; all other attacks affect them normally.
    • If all players in a team are in the backline, they are automatically considered 'Frontline'.
  • ✋ Hand Limit: Players can hold up to 10 cards. If exceeded, discard down to 10 immediately.
  • 🎲 Initiative Roll: At the start of each round, roll a d20 to determine turn order. Reroll ties.

🏆 Victory Conditions

The game ends when only one player or team has HP remaining. The last standing player or team is the winner.

⚔️ Combat

🔄 Turn Structure

🎲 Initiative Roll: At the beginning of each round, players roll a d20 to determine the turn order. Ties are rerolled.
🃏 On Each Player’s Turn:

  • 🔄 Draw Phase: Draw up to 2 cards. If the hand exceeds 10 cards, discard immediately.
  • ⚡ Action Phase: Play cards targeting yourself or others based on card effects. Spend AP as indicated on the cards.
  • ⏱️ Timing:
    • Quick: Play during an opponent’s turn as a reaction.
    • Normal/Cast: Play on your turn as standard actions.
  • ⏹️ End of Turn: Unused AP does not carry over. The next player begins their turn.

🔍 Advanced Combat Mechanics

🛡️ Damage System and Flow

Damage involves calculations of attack power, defense, block, and success hits to determine the impact. Here's an example:

  1. Damage Dealt: A card like Malefic Bond deals 40 damage.
  2. Defense Reduction: The target has 20 defense, reducing damage to 20 (40 - 20 = 20).
  3. Block Application: The target has 10 block, reducing damage to 10 (20 - 10 = 10).
  4. Success Hit: The remaining 10 damage reduces the target’s HP.

🎯 Targeting Defense and Specialized Defenses

  • Targeting Defense: Some cards, like Divine Strike, deal damage directly to defense without affecting HP, weakening defenses for follow-up attacks.
  • Specialized Defenses: Certain defenses block specific damage types. For example, Enchanted Armor blocks only magical damage.

⏰ Effect Resolution Timing

Start of Turn: Effects resolve in this order: AP buffs, AP reductions, AP debuffs, AP regeneration, defense buffs, defense debuffs, and crowd control effects (e.g., stuns).
End of Turn: Health-related effects resolve, including healing-over-time (HoT) and damage-over-time (DoT) effects.
