Necromancer

Deck Mechanics and Gameplay

Deck Mechanics and Gameplay

The Necromancer deck thrives on manipulating the graveyard and summoning powerful minions to dominate the battlefield. Cards in the deck synergize with discarded cards, allowing the Necromancer to recycle resources or fuel devastating abilities. Minion cards act as persistent effects, requiring careful management to maximize their impact while maintaining control of the board. Gameplay focuses on building a growing undead army, leveraging the discard pile for advantage, and overwhelming opponents with relentless pressure and strategic recursion.

Description

The Necromancer wields the power of death, summoning walkers (minions) and manipulating the discard piles of allies and enemies alike. With a unique focus on reaping and sacrificing, this class excels at attrition warfare and battlefield control.

Pros:

Ability to summon and command a relentless army of walkers.
Strong discard pile manipulation for strategic resource management.
Effective at debuffing enemies and outlasting opponents.

Cons:

Relatively fragile, relying on walkers for defense and offense.
High reliance on resources, which can make early turns slower.
Vulnerable to burst damage if unable to set up defenses.

Class Guide

Early Game:

Focus: Begin summoning walkers and setting up discard pile manipulation.
Tips: Use cards like Rotting Walker and Lost Reaping to establish early control.

Mid Game:

Focus: Transition into managing your walkers effectively and leveraging discard pile effects.
Tips: Combine Harvest of the Fallen with Sacrificial Offering to sustain your HP and damage output.

Late Game:

Focus: Overwhelm your opponents with walkers and high-impact cards like Dark Rebirth or Plague Legion.
Tips: Time your ultimate cards, such as Eternal Convergence, to maximize their impact.
