Hunter

Deck Mechanics and Gameplay

Deck Mechanics and Gameplay

The Hunter deck combines ranged precision, tracking abilities, and the aid of animal companions to control the battlefield. Animal Companions act as reaction cards, triggering effects based on specific circumstances, while Tracking and Marking cards amplify damage and enhance other abilities. The Hunter’s arsenal provides varied attack types and utility options, allowing for adaptability in combat. Gameplay focuses on leveraging companions, exploiting marked targets, and maintaining control through strategic use of traps and ranged attacks.

Description

The Hunter is a versatile ranged class, adept at tracking and weakening targets while dealing consistent damage from a distance. With a toolkit centered on precision and traps, the Hunter excels at controlling the battlefield and punishing foes.

Pros:

Strong ranged attacks with high precision and damage.
Effective crowd control through traps and debuffs.
Can adapt to different scenarios with versatile abilities.

Cons:

Relatively fragile, relying on positioning and evasion for survival.
Requires setup to maximize effectiveness (e.g., placing traps or marking targets).
Struggles in prolonged melee engagements.

Class Guide

Early Game:

Focus: Establish control with traps and mark key targets. Use cards like Hunter’s Snare to slow enemy advances.
Tips: Prioritize setting up traps in areas enemies are likely to engage.

Mid Game:

Focus: Use ranged attacks and abilities to pick off weakened foes. Cards like Marked Shot allow you to deal bonus damage to marked targets.
Tips: Balance offense and defense. Use cards like Evasive Leap to reposition when threatened.

Late Game:

Focus: Unleash powerful combos by chaining marks and traps. High-impact cards like Explosive Trap can deal significant damage to multiple enemies.
Tips: Manage resources carefully to maintain momentum. Avoid overcommitting AP without securing a kill.

Playstyle Tips:

Positioning Matters: Stay at range to maximize damage and minimize risk. Use cards like Camouflage to evade enemy attacks.
Target Priority: Focus on marking high-priority targets to amplify your damage output.
Trap Placement: Anticipate enemy movement. Traps are most effective when placed in high-traffic areas or choke points.
Timing Your Burst: Save powerful cards like Predator’s Strike for moments when marks or traps can amplify their effects.
